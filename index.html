Welcome to Taniti — your tropical island getaway

A small Pacific island with sandy and rocky beaches, lush rainforests, and a mountainous interior with a small active volcano. Enjoy a safe harbor, rich culture, and growing visitor amenities.

• 10 restaurants (local fish & rice, American, Pan-Asian) • Lodging from hostels to a four-star resort • Beaches, rainforest hikes, volcano visits & more

Things to Do

Beaches & Snorkeling

Swim, relax, or snorkel along the white sands around Yellow Leaf Bay. Charter fishing tours also available.

Rainforest & Volcano

Hike rainforest trails and visit the island’s small, active volcano for unforgettable views.

Culture & Nightlife

Local history museum, galleries, pub crawl (including a microbrewery), dance club, movies, bowling & arcade.

Tip: Merriton Landing (north side of Yellow Leaf Bay) hosts many activities and is easy to explore on foot.

Transportation

Arriving

  • Small airport (expansion planned for larger jets).
  • Small cruise ship docks one night per week at Yellow Leaf Bay.

Getting Around

  • Public buses in Taniti City (5 a.m.–11 p.m.). Private buses serve the rest of the island.
  • Taxis and local rental cars near the airport.
  • Bike rentals with helmets (required by law). City is flat and very walkable.

FAQ & Essentials

Safety & Health

  • Violent crime is rare; watch for petty theft as tourism grows.
  • One hospital with multilingual staff; several clinics available.

Money & Power

  • USD used widely; many accept euros and yen; major credit cards accepted.
  • Power outlets 120V (U.S. standard).

Language & Customs

  • Many younger locals speak English; less common in rural areas and among older residents.
  • Alcohol cannot be sold/served from midnight–9 a.m.; drinking age is 18 (not strictly enforced).
  • Many attractions and restaurants close on national holidays.
